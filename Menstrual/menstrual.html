The Menstrual Phase

Days 1-5 | Inner Winter

This is when your uterine lining sheds. Your estrogen levels rise slightly while progesterone, iron and energy levels are at their lowest. Ultimately, you have less energy, so this is the best time to rest and relax.

Nutrition

Cook meals that replenish your iron and support energy levels
Iron-Rich Foods: spinach, kale, lentils, red meat, eggs. Foods rich in vitamin C to help with iron absorption: oranges, bell peppers, strawberries. Healthy Fats: avocados, walnuts, chia seeds. Hydration Boosters: coconut water, herbal teas (chamomile and ginger helps most with cramps). Anti-Inflammatory Spices: tumeric, ginger.

Herbs & Teas

The best herbs and teas to support your period:
Cramp bark, ginger, chamomile, rapsberry leaf tea (to soothe the uterus and help reduce bleeding), nettle, valerian and willow bark.

Self-Care

Rest and gentle care
Add magnesium flakes to your bath to relax cramps and sore muscles, wear cozy pj’s, rest, journal, do some self reflection, nap, meditate, use a heating pad to help with cramps, get a massage, pamper yourself and write in a grattiude journal.

Movement

Light workouts
Movement should be very light as your energy is at it’s lowest during this phase. Exercises include yoga, mobility stretching, long walks and gentle pilates.

Lifestyle & Productivity

Rest, relax and reflect
Since your energy levels are at their lowest, it’s time to focus on resting and reflecting. Complete simple tasks, do low intensity and impact workouts, meditate, write in a grattitude journal and work on solo projects.
