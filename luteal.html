The Luteal Phase

Days 18 - 28 | Inner Autumn

Progesterone is produced and estrogen and testosterone levels dip. Your body prepares for menstruation again. You might feel unmotivated and you’re prone to fatigue. It's best to slow down, focus on self-care and work on your mobility. You’re mental clarity is improved and you have better emotional regulation.

Nutrition

These help ease PMS symptoms and maintain stable blood sugar
Magnesium-Rich Foods: dark chocolate, almonds, spinach, pumpkin seeds. Complex Carbs: brown rice, sweet potato, oats, whole grain bread. Calcium-Rich Foods: yogurt, almonds, cheese, fortified plant milk, cow milk. Hormone-Balancing: avocados, sesame seeds, sunflower seeds. Anti-Inflammatory: tumeric, fatty fish, cabbage, onion, white beans, turkey, apples, cod. Fiber-Rich: lentils, brussel sprouts.

Herbs & Teas

The best herbs and teas for the luteal phase:
Passionflower, skullcap, ginger and tumeric (anti-inflammatory and soothing), red clover, chasteberry, evening primrose oil, peppermint tea and chamomile for relaxation.

Self-Care

Mood regulation is key
Take warm baths, stretch, nap, watch a movie, light scented candles for aromatherapy, help regulate your mood with a comfort playlist.

Movement

Light weight training
Since your energy is slightly decreasing, it’s best to do moderate workouts to build aerobic endurance and relieve stress like light weight training, gentle yoga, walking/inclined walking, swimming, jogging and light stretching to ease tension.

Lifestyle & Productivity

As energy levels start to dip, it’s time to slow down and wrap things up.
Finish projects, clean up tasks, avoid new projects or big launches, focus on mundane or repetitive tasks like organising files or updating reports. Organise your space, journal, avoid caffeine, take long walks, set smaller goals and detox before the new cycle starts.
