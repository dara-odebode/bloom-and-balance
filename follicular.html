The Follicular Phase

Days 6 - 10 | Inner Spring

Your body starts to prepare for ovulation. Energy levels rise slightly, along with motivation, creativty and brainpower, making it a great time to begin new projects and focus on total body strength. You’re more optimistic, fresh, clear headed and your body’s resiliense to stress is great!

Nutrition

Focus on boosting energy and hormone production
Leafy greens and cruciferous vegetables like broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage and kale. Whole grains: oats and quinoa. Lean protein: chicken and turkey. Avocado, and olive oil for hormonal balance. Flax, chia, sunflower and pumpkin seeds. Lentils, chick peas, fermented foods, salmon, eggs, sweet potatoes, almonds, rasberries, blueberries and dark cholocate.

Herbs & Teas

The best herbs and teas for the follicular phase:
The best herbs and teas to support the follicular phase are rhodiola, chasteberry, ashwaganda, dandelion root, alfalfa, green tea, red raspberry tea, nettle, shatavari, goji berries, red clover blossoms and lemon balm.

Self-Care

Set goals and be creative
Read a good book, take a brisk walk, set new goals, practice positive affirmations make arts and crafts since you’re more creative in this phase, do yoga, and yoni steam to release dead cells and bacteria from the previous cycle.

Movement

Low to medium intensity workouts
As your energy levels rise, this is a good time to increase cardio and strength. Light to medium intensity workouts like running, cycling, pilates, full body workouts, HIIT cardio, inclined walking, resistance training and hiking are best for the luteal phase.

Lifestyle & Productivity

Learn new things and keep track of your progress
As your energy begins to rise, your productivity, brainpower, creativity, and optimism rises with it. This phase is a great time to start new projects, get creative, learn a new skill, set goals, make plans, journal to visualise progress, increase workout intensity and do focused work or learning. It’s also good to get into researching, online courses or writing. Write about things that inspire you, read a good book, brainstorm and try new things!
